The film follows the routine life of Hirayama, who cleans public restrooms and during his breaks, relaxes by having a bite to eat on a park bench and taking photos of the sun shining through the leaves above. I have since learned that the word in Japanese for the appreciation of sunlight coming through a canopy of leaves overhead is komorebi (木漏れ日). I have a trove of photos like that, so it should have its own word.

I also learned the movie, although directed by Wim Wenders,

> was born from the initiative of Yanai Kōji of Uniqlo, the fast retailing global chain, and the novelist/creative director Takasaki Takuma... (nippon.com)
