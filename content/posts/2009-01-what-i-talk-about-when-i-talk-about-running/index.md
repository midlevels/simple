Haruki Murakami wrote _What I Talk About When I Talk About Running_ in 2007. Combining reflections on running with personal anecdotes, the book provides insights into Murakami's experiences as a writer and a long-distance runner.

Throughout the memoir, Murakami shares how running has been an integral part of his life and creative process. He discusses his motivations for running, the discipline it requires, and the meditative aspects that allow him to find solace and inspiration.

As Murakami reflects on his own running journey, he contemplates the parallels between endurance running and the solitary nature of being a writer. He explores the mental and physical challenges he faces during long-distance races and draws connections to the mental stamina and persistence needed to write novels.

The memoir also delves into Murakami's personal life, revealing his routines, diet, and training methods. He discusses his experiences participating in marathons and triathlons, offering a glimpse into the physical and emotional aspects of endurance sports.

What I Talk About When I Talk About Running is not solely focused on running but also touches on broader themes such as aging, self-discipline, and the pursuit of one's passions. Murakami's introspective and reflective writing style invites readers to contemplate the deeper meaning and significance of physical activity and how it can shape one's life.
