_Riding Toward Everywhere_ delves into the history and culture of train hopping. Train hopping has its roots in the Great Depression era when many people hopped trains in search of work. Vollmann examines the allure of train hopping as a way to escape society's constraints and experience a sense of freedom and adventure. He also explores the dangers and risks inherent in this lifestyle. Risks such as encounters with law enforcement, injuries, and the constant uncertainty of where the next ride will take him.

Vollmann's writing is vivid and atmospheric, capturing the sights, sounds, and sensations of train hopping. He describes the landscapes he passes through and the camaraderie among train hoppers. He examines surviving in an unpredictable environment. His prose delves into the existential aspects of his journey, reflecting on the nature of freedom. He juxtaposes societal norms with the unconventional lifestyle of train hopping.

Vollmann grapples with his privilege and the ethical implications of documenting and participating in the lives of those he encounters. He examines the complexities of poverty, addiction, and mental health issues. These are the issues that often afflict train hoppers, shedding light on the harsh realities they face.

_Riding Toward Everywhere_ is not just a travelogue; it is a deeply personal and introspective exploration of the human condition. Vollmann's encounters with individuals from different walks of life offer a glimpse into the diversity and resilience of the human spirit. The book challenges readers to question societal norms, confront their own preconceptions, and reconsider the meaning of freedom and adventure.
