Hernan Diaz's novel *Trust* intricately weaves multiple narratives to explore themes of wealth, power, and the nature of truth. A tale that revolves around a wealthy financier, Benjamin Rask, and his enigmatic wife, Helen. The novel is structured in four parts: two contrasting narratives about Benjamin and Helen, a fictional biography, and an account of their financial dealings.

As the characters' perspectives unfold, the reader encounters differing accounts of their lives and relationships, prompting questions about the reliability of storytelling and the subjective nature of reality. The novel delves into the complexities of trust—both in personal relationships and in the broader context of societal and economic systems. Through its layered storytelling, Trust examines how narratives can shape and sometimes distort our understanding of the past.
