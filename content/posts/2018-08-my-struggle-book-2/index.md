The narrative of _My Struggle Book 2_ unfolds through a series of interconnected vignettes, alternating between past and present. Knausgaard reflects on his strained relationship with his father, who is depicted as a distant and mysterious figure. He delves into his insecurities and the challenges he faces as he tries to find his place in the world.

One of the themes of _My Struggle Book 2_ is Knausgaard's struggle to reconcile personal freedom and artistic expression with the responsibilities of family life. He marries and becomes a father. But he finds himself torn between the demands of domesticity and his need for solitude and creative fulfillment. Knausgaard's introspective and detailed writing style allows readers to intimately experience his emotions and thoughts as he grapples with these conflicting desires.

In addition to exploring his journey, Knausgaard also reflects on broader societal issues. He delves into the impact of globalization and the erosion of traditional Norwegian industries. The key industry is fishing, which affects the communities he encounters. Vivid descriptions of the rugged landscapes and the lives of those living in remote areas come into focus.

Knausgaard writes with raw honesty and unflinching self-examination. He delves into the minutiae of his experiences, often describing mundane details with meticulous attention. This approach allows him to capture the essence of everyday life and the complexities of human relationships.
